16th Note Strumming

Learn to strum with greater freedom


lets quickly revise 8th note patterns. We always:


1. Strum down on the beat (1, 2, 3, 4).

2. Keep our hand moving evenly and consistently.

3. Strum up on the 'ands' (off-beats).






The basic rules for 16th note strumming are as follows:


1. Strum down on the beat (1,2,3,4).

2.Keep our hand moving evenly and consistently.

3. Strum down on the 'ands' (off-beats).





As you can see the big difference is what is happening on the 'and', in 8th note strumming this was played with an up strum and in 16th note strumming it is played with a down.


Having a down strum on the beat and on the off beat means there must be an up in between each, so there are now two more divisions of the beat - giving as a total of 16 possible strums in a bar (hence 16th note strumming!).





Keep practicing this basic pattern. Get comfortable with it - 16th note strumming, more than anything elese, will seriously level up your rhythm guitar playing!



Building Barre Chords on the 4th, 5th, and 6th Strings



In music, a barre chord is a type of chord on a guitar or other stringed instrument played by using one or more fingers to press down multiple strings across a single fret of the fingerboard (like a bar pressing down the strings).Players often use this chording technique to play a chord that is not restricted by the tones of the guitar's open strings.

The barre's purpose is to move the open strings on the fretboard so you can move your fingers easily. One shape can let you play every major chord by merely shifting your fingers down a fret for each step.

You're mainly concerned with the E, Em, A, and Am shapes. the other chords are nice harmonic chords it would be well to learn, but these 4 are the main ones you start out with. Here are your first barre chords:





These are, from top left to bottom right, the F major, F minor, B♭ major, and B♭ minor chords. These chords can be moved around up and down the fretboard. For example, if you moved your B♭ minor chord up one fret (so your finger is barring the 2nd fret), you would now have a B minor chord - that is the power of barre chords!
